Guanacaste (Enterolobium cyclocarpum)

Characteristics

Impressive tree that can reach up to 35 meters in height, with a broad crown and compound leaves.

Habitat and Distribution

Found in low and dry areas from Mexico to South America; it is the national tree of Costa Rica.

Behavior

Deciduous; sheds its leaves during the dry season to conserve water.

Conservation Status

Not threatened.

Where to find it?

Common in open areas and national parks of the region. Notable tourist reserves in Nicoya: Barra Honda National Park and Camaronal Wildlife Refuge.

Fun Facts

  • Its name comes from the shape of its fruits, which resemble elephant ears.
