Green Iguana (Iguana iguana)

Characteristics

Large herbivorous lizard, bright green in juveniles and darker with age; features a prominent dorsal crest and a well-developed dewlap.

Habitat and Distribution

Found from Mexico to Brazil, including all of Costa Rica; inhabits tropical forests, riverine areas, and even urban zones.

Behavior

Diurnal and arboreal; feeds on leaves, flowers, and fruits; descends to the ground to bask and lay eggs.

Conservation Status

Least Concern (LC) according to the IUCN, but threatened by habitat loss and hunting.

Where to find it?

Common throughout Costa Rica, especially in vegetated areas near bodies of water. Notable tourist reserves in Nicoya: Karen Mogensen Nature Reserve, Curu Reserve, and Cabo Blanco.

Fun Facts

  • It is a popular species in the pet trade, leading to introductions outside its native range.
