Howler Monkey or Congo (Alouatta palliata)

Characteristics

Black fur, prehensile tail, emits very loud howls (up to 5 km).

Habitat and distribution

From Mexico to Ecuador, common on both slopes of Costa Rica.

Behavior

Groups of 10–20, led by a dominant male. Herbivorous.

Conservation status

Least Concern.

Where to find it?

Almost all Costa Rican national parks. Notable reserves in Nicoya: Karen Mogensen Nature Reserve, Curú Reserve, and Cabo Blanco

Fun facts

  • Their howls serve to mark territory, especially at dawn.
