Common Black Hawk (Buteogallus anthracinus)

Characteristics

Medium-sized raptor with black plumage and a distinctive white band on the tail.

Habitat and Distribution

Found in coastal areas and mangroves of Costa Rica, including the Nicoya Peninsula.

Behavior

Hunts by walking along water edges and through mangroves; feeds mainly on crabs and other invertebrates.

Conservation Status

Least Concern (LC) according to the IUCN.

Where to find it?

Mangroves and coastal zones of the Pacific, such as the Tempisque River basin. Notable tourist reserves in Nicoya: Karen Mogensen Nature Reserve

Fun Facts

  • Its crab-based diet has earned it the nickname "crab hawk" in some regions.
