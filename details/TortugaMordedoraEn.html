Snapping Turtle (Chelydra serpentina)

Characteristics

Large, robust turtle with a big head, long neck, and a long spiny tail.

Habitat and distribution

Native to North America but reported in some parts of Central America, including Costa Rica, possibly as an introduced species.

Behavior

Aquatic and aggressive when threatened; feeds on fish, amphibians, and plant matter.

Conservation status

Least Concern (LC) according to the IUCN.
