Rattlesnake (Crotalus durissus)

Characteristics

Venomous snake of medium to large size, with a diamond pattern on its back and a rattle at the tip of its tail.

Habitat and Distribution

Lives in savannas, dry forests, and open areas from Mexico to South America. In Costa Rica, it is rare and localized.

Behavior

Terrestrial and mainly nocturnal; feeds on small mammals and birds.

Conservation Status

Least Concern (LC) according to the IUCN.

Where to find it?

Dry and open regions of northern Costa Rica, although it is uncommon. Notable tourist reserves in Nicoya: Karen Mogensen Nature Reserve, Curu Reserve, and Cabo Blanco.

Fun Facts

  • Its venom is neurotoxic and can be dangerous to humans; however, encounters are rare due to its shy behavior.
