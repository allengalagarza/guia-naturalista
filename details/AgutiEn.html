Central American Agouti (Dasyprocta punctata)

Characteristics

Robust rodent (2–4 kg), reddish-brown fur, very short tail.

Habitat and Distribution

Tropical and secondary forests in Central and South America. Very common in Costa Rica.

Behavior

Diurnal and shy; feeds on fruits and seeds, burying them (important seed disperser).

Conservation Status

Least Concern.

Where to find it?

Forested areas and national park trails. Notable tourist reserves in Nicoya: Karen Mogensen Nature Reserve, Curu Reserve, and Cabo Blanco.

Fun Facts

  • It can “bury food” like squirrels, helping regenerate the forest.
