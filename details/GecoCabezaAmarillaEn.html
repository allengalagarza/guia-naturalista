Yellow-headed Gecko (Gonatodes albogularis)

Characteristics

Small gecko with sexual dimorphism; males have a bright yellow head and dark body, while females are uniformly brown.

Habitat and Distribution

Found in Central and South America, including Costa Rica; inhabits dry and humid forests as well as urban areas.

Behavior

Diurnal and territorial; insectivorous; females lay eggs in communal sites such as tree bases and leaf litter.

Conservation Status

Least Concern (LC) according to the IUCN.
