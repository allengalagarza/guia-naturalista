Roseate Spoonbill (Platalea ajaja)

Characteristics

Wading bird with pink plumage and a long, flat, spoon-shaped bill.

Habitat and Distribution

Common in the Tempisque Basin and the Gulf of Nicoya during the dry season; also found in other Pacific coastal areas.

Behavior

Feeds by slowly walking in shallow waters, sweeping its bill side to side to catch prey.

Conservation Status

Least Concern (LC) according to the IUCN.

Where to find it?

Mangroves and estuaries of the Gulf of Nicoya, especially in areas like Palo Verde. Notable tourist reserves in Nicoya: Curu Reserve and Cabo Blanco.

Fun Facts

  • Its pink color comes from a carotenoid-rich diet, similar to flamingos.
