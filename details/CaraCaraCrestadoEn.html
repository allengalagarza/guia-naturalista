Crested Caracara (Caracara plancus)

Characteristics

Large raptor with a prominent crest; black, white, and orange plumage.

Habitat and Distribution

Common in the dry northwest of Costa Rica up to 750 m in altitude; also present along the Pacific slope to the south.

Behavior

Opportunistic; feeds on carrion and small animals; often seen walking in open fields.

Conservation Status

Least Concern (LC) according to the IUCN.

Where to find it?

Open and semi-open areas of northern and central Pacific Costa Rica. Notable tourist reserves in Nicoya: Karen Mogensen Nature Reserve and Curu Reserve.

Fun Facts

  • Also known as "bone-carrying caracara" due to its habit of feeding on bone remains.
