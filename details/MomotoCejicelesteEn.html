Turquoise-browed Motmot (Eumomota superciliosa)

Characteristics

Colorful bird with turquoise brows, green back, and a long tail with racket-like tips.

Habitat and Distribution

Common in open habitats such as forest edges and semi-open areas from southeastern Mexico to Costa Rica.

Behavior

Diurnal and non-migratory; feeds on insects and small reptiles; often seen perched on exposed branches.

Conservation Status

Least Concern (LC) according to the IUCN.

Where to find it?
