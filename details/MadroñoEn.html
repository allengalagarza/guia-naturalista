Madroño (Calycophyllum candidissimum)

Characteristics

Medium-sized tree with fragrant white flowers that bloom during the dry season.

Habitat and Distribution

Dry and semi-deciduous forests from Mexico to South America.

Behavior

Blooms in the dry season, providing nectar for pollinators.

Conservation Status

Not threatened.

Where to find it?

Common in the Nicoya Peninsula. Notable tourist reserves in Nicoya: Cabo Blanco Absolute Natural Reserve and also seen at Curú Wildlife Refuge.

Fun Facts

  • In Nicaragua, its branches are used to decorate altars during religious festivities.
