Gumbo-Limbo (Bursera simaruba)

Characteristics

Medium-sized tree recognized for its reddish, smooth bark that peels off easily.

Habitat and Distribution

Dry forests from the southern United States to South America.

Behavior

Well adapted to poor soils and dry conditions.

Conservation Status

Not threatened.

Where to find it?

Common in the Nicoya Peninsula and other dry regions. Notable tourist reserves in Nicoya: Santa Rosa National Park and Cabo Blanco Absolute Natural Reserve.

Fun Facts

  • Its bark is used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.
